## Task 8 — Calibration-led positioning

### Taglines (pick one)
- **Know what you actually know.**
- Study like a game. Pass like you calibrated.
- The exam rewards judgment. So does this.

### One-sentence pitch
> ACED is a roguelike study game that trains the skill the CPA exam actually scores — *knowing when you're
> right* — by making you bet your confidence on every question and resurfacing what you miss on a spaced schedule.

### Homepage block (calibration-led)
> **Headline:** You don't fail FAR because you didn't study. You fail because you didn't know what you didn't know.
>
> **Subhead:** ACED turns FAR into a roguelike run. Every hand, you *call your confidence* before you answer —
> and honesty is scored. Overconfidence is the #1 silent killer on exam day; here it costs you points until you
> fix it. Miss a question and it comes back on a spaced schedule until it sticks. Free to play, no signup.
>
> **CTA:** ▶ Play today's Daily Close (5 questions, ~2 min) · Start a full run

### Why-it's-worth-paying line (for the pricing section)
> A review course teaches you the material. ACED tells you — brutally, daily — whether it actually stuck. That
> gap is where scores are lost, and it's the cheapest thing you can fix.

### Three content angles (blog / social / lead magnet)
1. **"The silent defaults FAR keeps testing."** The unsaid word in a stem that triggers a rule and baits a
   specific wrong answer. (You already ship the cheat sheet — this is the evergreen SEO + share piece.)
2. **"80% sure, 55% right: the calibration gap that fails candidates."** Teach the concept, then show ACED
   scoring it. This is the wedge no competitor owns.
3. **"The 5-minute Daily Close."** One shared question set per day, same for everyone, a spoiler-free share
   grid, and a one-tap friend challenge. The habit *is* the marketing.

## Task 10 — Community contribution plan (2 weeks)

**Rule:** contribute value first; never drop links cold. Communities (r/CPA, CPA Discords, studygram, #cpaexam
on TikTok) punish ads and reward genuinely useful regulars. Post *as a candidate/builder who made a free thing*,
not as a brand.

**Cadence:** ~20–30 min/day. Reply to 3 threads for every 1 post of your own.

| Day | Action |
|-----|--------|
| 1 | r/CPA: answer 3 "how do I retain FAR" threads with a real, specific tip (no link). Establish presence. |
| 2 | Post the **free Silent-Defaults cheat sheet** as a standalone resource ("made this for my own studying, sharing it"). Link only if the sub allows resources. |
| 3 | Join 2 CPA Discords. Introduce yourself, answer questions in study channels. No links yet. |
| 4 | studygram/TikTok: short post on *one* silent default (carousel or 20s clip). Value, not promo. |
| 5 | r/CPA: a "calibration" insight post — why feeling ready ≠ being ready — with a concrete self-test. |
| 6 | Reply day. Help in Discords; note recurring pain points (fuel for content). |
| 7 | Share your own Daily Close result grid (spoiler-free) + friend-challenge link in a Discord study channel: "anyone want to beat my Close today?" |
| 8 | Rest / engage lightly. |
| 9 | r/CPA: post the **daily challenge** framed as a group habit ("doing a 5-min FAR Close every morning, link inside if you want to join"). |
| 10 | TikTok/IG: "I bet my confidence on 5 FAR questions every day — here's what it taught me." |
| 11 | Answer threads; DM 2 people who engaged, offer to study-buddy via daily challenges. |
| 12 | Post angle #2 (the 80/55 calibration gap) as a mini-thread. |
| 13 | Discord: run a small "Close of the day" leaderboard for a study group. |
| 14 | Review what landed (saves, shares, click-throughs). Double down on the format that worked; drop the rest. |

**What to measure:** saves/upvotes (does the value land?), click-throughs to the cheat sheet, and how many
people actually play a Daily Close from a challenge link (that's your K-factor signal).
